1. AGE

    Age is a significant risk factor for cardiovascular diseases. The likelihood of developing heart disease increases with age.

  2. Sex

    There are differences in heart disease prevalence and symptoms between males and females.

  3. Chest Pain

    Refers to discomfort or pain in the chest, often a symptom of heart disease, including angina, myocardial infarction, or other cardiac conditions.

  4. Resting Blood Pressure

    The pressure exerted by circulating blood on the walls of blood vessels when the heart is at rest, measured in mm Hg.

  5. Serum Cholesterol

    Serum cholesterol measures the total cholesterol level in the blood, including LDL (bad) and HDL (good) cholesterol. High levels can increase the risk of heart disease and stroke.

  6. Fasting Blood Sugar

    The blood glucose level measured after fasting for at least 8 hours. Elevated levels can indicate diabetes, a significant risk factor for cardiovascular diseases and other health issues.

  7. Resting Electrocardiographic Results

    The findings from an ECG taken while at rest, assessing heart rhythm and electrical activity. Abnormal results can indicate conditions like ischemia, arrhythmias, or left ventricular hypertrophy.

  8. Maximum Heart Rate Achieved

    The highest heart rate recorded during a stress test, indicating cardiovascular fitness. It helps evaluate the heart's response to physical exertion and detect potential heart conditions.

  9. Exercise-Induced Angina

    Exercise-induced angina is chest pain or discomfort that occurs during physical activity or exercise. It happens because the heart muscle isn't getting enough oxygen-rich blood to meet the increased demands during exertion. This can be a sign of underlying coronary artery disease or other heart conditions.
